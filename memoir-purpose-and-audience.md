# Phase 0: Purpose & Audience

The original pipeline started at Recall — but *what* you recall, *how* you structure it,
and *how openly* you write all depend on a question the writer should answer first:

> **Who is this for, and what is it really about?**

This short phase happens once, before serious recall begins. It is owned by the
**Architect** skill (which writes the result to `memories/style_guide.md`), but any
skill can run it if the writer arrives without having set direction.

---

## The Three Questions

### 1. Who is the reader?
This single answer changes everything downstream:

| Reader | Implications |
|--------|--------------|
| **Family / descendants** (most common) | Names and private detail are usually fine; the goal is preservation and connection. Less legal worry, more emphasis on warmth and honesty with relatives. |
| **The public / publication** | Strangers must be made to *care*; theme and universality matter more; real-people and legal care (see `memoir-ethics-and-care.md`, Part 2) tightens. |
| **Yourself / healing** | No external audience; permission to be raw and unresolved; "done" is defined by the writer's own sense of completion, not polish. |

Ask directly: *"Before we dig into memories — who do you picture reading this? Your
grandchildren, a general reader, or is this mainly for you?"*

### 2. What is the scope?
- **Whole-life** — birth to now. Risk: thin and list-like. Needs ruthless selection.
- **A single thread** (a relationship, an illness, an immigration, a career, a year) —
  usually the *stronger* memoir. Memoir is depth, not coverage.
- **A collection of vignettes** — standalone moments, lightly linked. Good for family
  keepsakes and for writers who want results fast.

Recommend, gently, that focused beats comprehensive: *"The strongest memoirs are usually
about one thing seen deeply, not everything seen briefly. Is there a single thread
pulling at you?"*

### 3. What is it *about* (the "so what")?
The hardest and most important question. A memoir is not a sequence of events; it is
those events *meaning something*.
- "If a stranger finished this book, what would you want them to understand or feel?"
- "What question is this book really wrestling with?" (e.g. *Can you forgive a parent
  you also miss? What do you owe the place you left?*)

It's fine if the answer is fuzzy at the start — but revisit it. The theme often clarifies
after recall, and the Architect should circle back to it.

---

## Output

Record the answers at the top of `memories/style_guide.md` under a **Project Compass**
section, so every later phase can see them:

```markdown
## 0. Project Compass
*   **Reader**: [Family / Public / Self]
*   **Scope**: [Whole-life / Single thread: ___ / Vignettes]
*   **About (working theme)**: [One sentence — what this book is really wrestling with]
*   **Privacy stance**: [Real names / Some names changed / TBD] (see memoir-ethics-and-care.md)
```

This Compass is a living note — update it as the book reveals itself. The **Architect**
checks it before structuring; the **Writer** checks it for tone; the **Revision** skill
checks the finished draft against it ("Does this actually deliver on its compass?").
